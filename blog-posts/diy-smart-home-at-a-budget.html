DIY Smart Home at a Budget

  • Home
  • Blog
  • DIY Smart Home at a Budget
Jessica G5 Apr 2021

DIY setup of your smart home system is not that expensive or difficult. And the best part of this guide is, it is renter friendly. You can just unplug everything and bring them all with you when you decide to move out.

Ecosystem

I recommend using Google for your ecosystem due to reasonable price of its smart home products, and easy to setup.

Pre-requisites

You would need to make sure your internet connection and speed is good, wi-fi signal is strong enough to reach the devices. That’s all you need honestly. And of course, electricity to power up your smart devices.

Setting Up a Hub

Plugin and setup your account in your hub, in Google’s case is the Google Nest Hub. If you already have a Google account, setup is as easy as logging into your account on the hub. You want to also install the Google Home app on your mobile for remote control.

Smart Lights

Philips Hue is recommended due to easy setup and good product availability. Choose your light type (ES/downlight/etc.) and get the Hue Bridge. Some indoor or outdoor motion sensors would be great additions as well. Connect Hue to Google through the Google Home app, pairing the Hue Bridge with your Google Home system.

Entertainment

Your Google Nest Hub speaker is not that bad, but Google Nest Mini or Google Nest Audio is the one you want to look at for speaker. You can also connect other smart speakers that are compatible with Google system.

Chromecast is handy for streaming. You can just use your voice now to tell your smart TV to play your favourite show or to control the volume.

Home Security

Google has three types of camera available for Australian market at the moment. Two types for indoor use, and one type for outdoor. They are all need to be connected to main power. Google Nest cameras have great video quality, but the outdoor camera may not be renter friendly as it requires to be plugged into a power point. A good alternative is Ring Spotlight Cam Battery or Arlo Pro 3, although you still need to mount it on the wall.
