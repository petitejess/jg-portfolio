Online Journal for Your Team

  • Home
  • Blog
  • Online Journal for Your Team
Jessica G18 Apr 2021

Looking for an online journal to keep your projects running efficiently? Now, we have several options to choose from. In this article, I would compare some of the popular project management tools we can find today.

Brand Options

There are many options of online project management tools available that you can find today, but we will be looking at a more generic ones. Unless your projects are software development projects (Jira is good for that), tools like Trello and Microsoft Planner would be powerful enough to manage your team.

Features Comparison

Trello is a management tool in a form of a subscription based web application which can be used collaboratively by all staff members within all departments to manage, to organise and to keep track of business operational activities, tasks, projects and plans. Microsoft Planner is a project management tool designed for small teams, to manage personal and teamwork plans.

In comparison, feature wise, Trello and Microsoft Planner are similar. Both products manage daily tasks/projects/plan in a form of boards that consist of smaller tasks. Other capabilities, such as user accountability and access control are pretty similar. However, currently, Microsoft Planner has one feature that Trello is lacking, an event scheduling. This is useful if you frequently launch products or hold events.

If you like Trello more, but needed the event scheduling feature from Microsoft Planner, you could integrate Trello with G Suite. You can then use Google event scheduling instead.

Price Comparison

At the time of writing this article, Trello offers a free plan for individuals and small teams. The other plan available is $10 per user per month with annual commitment, suitable for teams up to 100 members that are working on multiple projects. The enterprise plan, suitable for large companies with more than 100 people working on various projects would need to contact their sales team for pricing.

Microsoft Planner also has three options available for us to choose. The difference from Trello is that all plans in Microsoft Planner have a maximum number of user of 300. The first option is the Basic plan for $6.90 per user per month with annual commitment. The second one is the Standard plan for $17.20 per user per month with annual commitment, which includes Microsoft Office desktop apps. The last option is the Premium plan for $27.50 per user per month with annual commitment, that has every features in Basic and Standard plans, plus advanced security features and device management features.

Complete details of their products can be found in their official websites.

Conclusion

If your team is small, I would recommend Trello for its value over features offered. It has the essential features needed to track tasks, projects, plans and activities. Trello also keeps logs of actions for accountability and to increase integrity of business activities. It can be integrated with G Suite.
